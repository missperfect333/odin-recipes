Jollof Rice

Home

Description

Jollof Rice is more than just a dish; it is a celebration of flavor, history, and culture. Known for its iconic reddish-orange hue and deep, smoky undertones, this one-pot masterpiece is the crown jewel of West African cuisine.

Ingredients

To Steam the Chicken

  • 1 Whole Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 Large Onion (blended)
  • 2 Thumb-sized pieces of Ginger (blended)
  • 2 Cloves Garlic (blended)
  • 1 Habanero Pepper (blended)
  • 1 tbsp Rosemary
  • 1/2 tsp Aniseed
  • All-Purpose Seasoning & Salt (to taste)

For the Stew Base

  • 2 Medium Red Onions & 1 Medium Yellow Onion (sliced)
  • 10 Habanero Peppers (blended)
  • 1/2 Bulb of Garlic (blended)
  • 1 Thumb-sized piece of Ginger (blended)
  • 3 Bay Leaves & 1 Star Anise
  • 3 lbs 10 oz Canned Tomatoes (or fresh)
  • 2 cups Tomato Paste
  • 1/4 cup Chicken Powder
  • 1 tbsp Salt & 1/2 tbsp Rosemary
  • 1 tbsp Smoked Paprika
  • 1/4 cup All-Purpose Seasoning
  • Curry Powder, Thyme, and Ground Anise (to taste)

Steps

  1. Place chicken in a pot. Add the blended onion, ginger, garlic, and habanero. Season with rosemary, aniseed, salt, and all-purpose seasoning. Cover and steam until the chicken is tender. Reserve the stock!
  2. Remove chicken from the pot and deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil and fry the sliced red and yellow onions until softened. Add the bay leaves and star anise to let the oils become fragrant.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and fry for about 5–8 minutes. Add the canned/fresh tomatoes and the blended "pepper base" (habanero, garlic, ginger). Cook until the sauce thickens and oil begins to separate.
  5. Add the chicken powder, smoked paprika, curry powder, thyme, and ground anise. Stir in the reserved chicken stock from step 1 for maximum flavor.
  6. Add washed long-grain rice to the pot. Stir well to ensure every grain is coated in the stew. Add a little water if needed, but rely mostly on steam.
  7. Cover tightly (using foil under the lid helps) and cook on low heat for 30–40 minutes until the rice is fluffy and perfectly orange. Serve with your fried chicken and shito!
