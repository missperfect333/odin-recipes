Pistachio Cheesecake

Home

Description

This isn't just a dessert; it’s a well-engineered masterpiece. Our **Pistachio Cheesecake** features a buttery, toasted nut crust and a dense, silky-smooth filling infused with real roasted pistachio paste.

Ingredients

The Nutty Crust

  • 1 1/2 cups Graham cracker crumbs (or digestive biscuits)
  • 1/2 cup Roasted pistachios (finely ground)
  • 1/4 cup Granulated sugar
  • 6 tbsp Unsalted butter (melted)

The Cheesecake Filling

  • 24 oz Cream cheese (3 packs, softened to room temperature)
  • 1 cup Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup Heavy cream
  • 3 Large eggs (room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup Pure pistachio paste (unsweetened)
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp Lemon zest

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Mix the cracker crumbs, ground pistachios, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until perfectly smooth. Ensure there are no "lumps" in your logic—this is the foundation of the cake.
  3. Add the pistachio paste, vanilla, and lemon zest. Mix until the color is a uniform, beautiful pale green.
  4. Incorporate the eggs one at a time on low speed. Be careful not to "over-clock" the batter; adding too much air can cause cracks during baking!
  5. Pour the filling over the cooled crust. Wrap the bottom of the pan in foil and place it in a water bath (bain-marie). Bake for 55–65 minutes until the edges are set but the center still has a slight "jiggle."
  6. Turn off the oven and crack the door open. Let the cheesecake cool inside for an hour. Then, refrigerate for at least 6 hours (or overnight) to allow the structure to fully set.
  7. Before serving, top with extra crushed pistachios or a drizzle of white chocolate.
