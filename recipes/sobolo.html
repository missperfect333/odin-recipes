Sobolo (Hibiscus Tea/Drink)

Home

Description

Sobolo is the quintessential Ghanaian refresher. Made from dried hibiscus petals (locally known as bisaab), this drink is famous for its deep ruby color and its intense, spicy-sweet profile.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Dried Hibiscus petals (Bisaab)
  • 1 cup Fresh Ginger (crushed or blended)
  • 10-12 Hwentia (Grains of Selim)
  • 1 tbsp Cloves (Peprɛ)
  • 1 ripe Pineapple (peels for boiling, fruit for juice/garnish)
  • 2 sticks of Cinnamon
  • Sugar or honey (to taste)
  • 1 small gallon Water

Steps

  1. Thoroughly wash the dried hibiscus petals in cold water to remove any dust. Place them in a large pot with 1 gallon of water.
  2. Add the pineapple peels, crushed ginger, cloves, hwentia, and cinnamon sticks to the pot. These are the "core dependencies" that give Sobolo its signature kick.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil and let it simmer for 20–30 minutes. The water should turn a deep, dark purple-red.
  4. Turn off the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly. Use a fine-mesh strainer to separate the liquid from the petals and spices.
  5. While the liquid is still warm, stir in your sugar or honey. For an extra tropical "patch," stir in freshly squeezed pineapple juice.
  6. Pour the Sobolo into glass bottles and refrigerate until ice-cold.
  7. Serve over ice with a slice of lemon, orange, or a sprig of mint.
